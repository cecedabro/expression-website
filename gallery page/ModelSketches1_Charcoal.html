Real life model rapid charcoal sketches! - only 10 minutes per drawing [first model session]

This was the first time I ever did real life model sketches!

The medium was charcoal and we had 10 minutes per "pose".

Something to notice are the lines and proportions. The teacher had taught us a few quick things about basic proportions. A ground rule was "height of the body equals five times the head."

Another thing to notice is how some lines are tilted and others are straight. This was meant to guide the drawing process and help with drawing realistic posture.

Here's another sketch from the same model. I tried to make his fingers curl around the box he sat on, but I only had time to do it with his right hand.

I struggled maintaining good perspective with the model and box. Overall still glad how it turned out!
